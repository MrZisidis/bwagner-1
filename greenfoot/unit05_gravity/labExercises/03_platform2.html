Download and Open Scenario

  1. Click on the following link to download the Scenario: Platform2

  2. Select Open.

  3. Copy the folder Platform2 and paste it in your MyGreenfoot folder.

  4. Launch Greenfoot and select Open from the Scenario menu.

  5. Navigate to your MyGreenfoot folder, select the Platform2 Scenario, and click Open.

Method properPlatformLanding

When running the platform game in lesson 1 you might have noticed a problem when the frog attempted a "landing" on a platform. If any part of the frog's body was touching the top of the platform the frog made a successful landing on the platform. This problem is illustrated below where a frog makes a successful landing simple by touching the platform with the tip of its nose.

This problem is fixed by the method properPlatformLanding shown below.

This method uses the distance formula to calculate the distance between the center of the frog and the center of the platform. This distance is then used to calculate how much of the frog's body is on the platform. If at least 40% of the frog is on the platform the method returns true; otherwise it returns false. The method also returns false if the frog's center is not above the platform's center when the frog touches the platform.

Method platFormTest

To incorporate properPlatformLanding into the program we modify the platformTest method implemented in lesson 1.

Now if the frog does not land on the platform with at least 40% of its body touching the platform it will simply fall straight down.



Program Modifications

  1. Modify the MyWorld class constructor so that when the game starts the frog is on top of the middle platform.

  2. Add a new method to the Frog class named endGameTest. This method should cause the game to stop when the frog "falls" to the ground.

    Look in the documentation for the Greenfoot class to find a method you can use to stop the program (end the game).
